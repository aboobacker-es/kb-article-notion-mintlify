Email alerts for question leakage help you quickly identify and respond to leaked questions, maintaining the integrity and reliability of your hiring process.

## Key benefits

Email alerts provide the following benefits:

- **Managed alerts:** Receive notifications only for the tests you subscribe to, and set the email frequency to daily or weekly.
- **Easy leakage management:** Use the direct link in each email alert to open the affected test and replace the leaked question immediately.

## Enabling email alerts

You can enable email alerts for leaked questions at the company level, user level, or test level.

### Enabling email alerts for all company tests

<Note>
**Note:** You must have admin access to configure company-level settings.
</Note>

To enable email alerts for all company tests:

1. Log in to your **HackerRank for Work** account using your credentials.
1. Click your profile icon in the upper-right corner.
1. Select **Settings **from the drop-down menu.

All test owners and editors will receive an email notification when a question is leaked.

### Enabling email alerts for your tests

You can enable email alerts for all the tests you have created.

To enable email alerts for all your tests:

1. Click your profile icon in the upper-right corner.
1. Select **Settings **from the drop-down menu.
1. Go to **Email Notifications **under the **User **section.

  <Frame>
    <img src="/images/manage-email-alerts-for-leaked-questions/941f7b6b5907033d.bin" alt="image" />
  </Frame>
1. Toggle **Turn on leakage alerting for all my tests by default** to enable email alerts and select **daily** or **weekly** to receive alerts.

  <Frame>
    <img src="/images/manage-email-alerts-for-leaked-questions/8040fbfeca44ea71.bin" alt="image" />
  </Frame>
1. Click **Save**.

### Enabling email alerts for a specific test

You can enable email alerts for a specific test that you own or created for other users within your company.

To enable email alerts for a specific test:

1. Go to the **Tests** tab.
1. Select the test for which you want to enable email alerts.
1. Go to the **Settings **tab.
1. Select **Emails **under the **Test Administration **section.

  <Frame>
    <img src="/images/manage-email-alerts-for-leaked-questions/78996ff41ba78213.bin" alt="image" />
  </Frame>
1. Click the **Enable** dropdown under **Leakage Alert Emails**.
1. Select the users who should receive the email alerts.

  <Frame>
    <img src="/images/manage-email-alerts-for-leaked-questions/ed29c5ec6aeadb0a.bin" alt="image" />
  </Frame>
1. Click **Save Changes**.

## Receiving and responding to email alerts

After you enable alerts, test editors receive an email that includes:

- A list of tests with leaked questions.
- Direct calls to action (CTAs) to manage each affected test.
